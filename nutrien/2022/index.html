Does the company disclose financial risks related to drinking water contamination from leaching of applied products? Please return NONE if there is no disclosure at all, UNSURE if there is some mention of risk but no link to financial risk, PARTIAL if there is mention of financial risk, YES if there are numbers attached to financial risks disclosure.

Justification:
Nutrien discloses a financial penalty of $668,100 as part of an EPA settlement related to improper application of dicamba, a crop protection product. While not explicitly stating "drinking water contamination from leaching," this incident represents a direct financial risk and consequence stemming from issues related to the application of products, which the report broadly discusses as contributing to "Loss to water" through leaching and runoff. The specific monetary value qualifies the finding as YES.
Evidence:
Nutrien and EPA Resolution In 2021, Nutrien Ag Solutions agreed to settle claims made by the EPA, that the Nutrien Ag Solutions located in Galva, KS: (1) applied over-the-top dicamba in wind speeds higher than permitted, and (2) failed to keep records in acceptable condition. Nutrien Ag Solutions agreed to pay $668,100 and has taken the additional steps of conducting trainings on chemical application; working with applicators to comply fully with label requirements; and improving recordkeeping practices.

Nutrien acknowledges the risk that new types of seeds requiring fewer crop nutrients, or other technological developments leading to improved nutrient-use efficiency, could adversely affect the demand for their products and results of operations.
