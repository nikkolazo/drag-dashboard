Justification:

The company reports specific environmental provisions in millions of dollars, details the methodology for estimating these provisions, and provides examples of costs for clean-up and remediation activities at specific sites, acknowledging potential uncertainties and possible increases beyond current provisions.
Evidence:
Provisions for remediation costs are made when there is a present obligation, it is probable that expenditures for remediation work will be required within ten years (or a longer period if specified by a legal obligation) and the cost can be estimated within a reasonable range of possible outcomes. Environmental liabilities are recorded at the estimated amount at which the liability could be settled at the balance sheet date, and are discounted if the impact is material and if cost estimates and timing are considered reasonably certain. Syngenta‚Äôs restructuring programs have involved closure of several sites to date. Remediation liabilities recognized when site closures are announced are accounted for as restructuring provisions.
